Awards and Eligibility

  • Parent, stepparent or guardian is employed as a full-time employee of an eligible Brunswick Corporation dealership* and has been employed at that dealership for at least one year as of March 1, 2020.
  • Currently in final year of high school or a current college freshman, sophomore or junior.
  • Grade point average of 2.0 or higher on a 4.0 scale; or an average of 70% or higher.
  • Entering full-time into an accredited college, university or vocational school in Canada or the United States for the fall of 2020.
  • 23 years of age or younger (exception for military duty) as of March 1, 2020.
  • Students selected for this award will receive a single-year scholarship - $2,000 in the United States and Can$2,000 in Canada.
  • All scholarship winners will be individually notified by email of the committee's decision no later than May 1, 2020.
  • Dealership spouses are not eligible to apply
  • Dealership employees are eligible to apply as long as they meet ALL of the above-mentioned criteria.

Selection Process

Selections are made by an impartial, outside committee comprised of professionals from prominent universities. No representatives of Brunswick Corporation or The Brunswick Foundation are involved in the selection process. All selection committee decisions are confidential and final. There is no appeal process.

No preference is given to an applicant by reason of a parent’s position, length of employment, relationship with Brunswick or by the dealerships standing with Brunswick Corporation. Consideration for all awards is based on the applicant’s academic record, achievement test scores and leadership abilities as demonstrated by extracurricular activities and work record. Financial need is also an additional consideration for the awards.

Rules and Regulations

  • The application must be completed by the student applicant only (in English), otherwise the application will not be accepted.
  • Completing the online application does not guarantee that you will receive a scholarship.
  • Copies or prior scholarship application forms will not be accepted.
  • No materials will be returned back to the applicant.
  • Payments will be disbursed each July payable to the school and mailed directly to the institution.
  • The funds provided must be used only for educational expenses (e.g. tuition, books and fees).
  • The scholarship may not be used for graduate school or master’s degree.
  • Current year recipients that remain eligible may reapply each year and compete for a scholarship up to US $2,000 or Can$2,000 to receive a maximum of US $8,000 or Can$8,000 during their four years of undergraduate studies.
  • Scholarship recipients are required to complete a sufficient number of school credit hours each year to complete degree requirements within the normal time period.
  • It is the intent of The Brunswick Foundation (Brunswick Corporation in Canada) to avoid granting a student financial aid in excess of educational expenses. In accordance with the Internal Revenue Service regulations, financial aid in excess of actual education expenses may result in taxable income to the recipient.
  • Canada recipients will be issued a T4A tax slip reporting the amount of the award. Depending on the student’s circumstances, the scholarship award may be exempt from tax under the Income Tax Act (Canada). Recipients should seek independent tax advice regarding their circumstances.
  • Scholarship recipients are not required to accept employment with Brunswick Corporation or any of its related divisions/subsidiaries upon graduation.
  • The administration and financing of this program is exclusively the responsibility of The Brunswick Foundation, (Brunswick Corporation in Canada) which has full authority to make rules and regulations governing the program and to make final decisions in all matters arising in connection therewith except for the selection of scholarship recipients. The Foundation (Corporation in Canada) may modify or discontinue the program at any time. The application is property of The Brunswick Foundation (Brunswick Corporation in Canada).
